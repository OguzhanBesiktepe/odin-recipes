Doner Kebab

Original Source Material

Turkish döner kebab is a popular street food delicacy known for its savory flavors and unique preparation method, holding a special place in the hearts of both locals and tourists alike. The dish consists of meat, traditionally lamb, beef, or chicken, which is seasoned with a rich blend of spices, then stacked in the shape of an inverted cone and slowly roasted on a vertical rotisserie. As the outer layer cooks to a perfect crispness, thin slices are shaved off and served, ensuring each bite is succulently tender with a slightly crispy edge. This meat can be tucked into a warm, fluffy pita or lavash bread, accompanied by a variety of fillings such as fresh lettuce, tomato, onion, and a drizzle of sauces like garlic yogurt or spicy tomato. The result is a harmonious blend of textures and flavors, making döner kebab a satisfying meal at any time of the day.

The origins of döner kebab date back to the 19th century Ottoman Empire, evolving into a beloved fast-food staple across the globe, especially in urban areas with vibrant Turkish communities. Its appeal lies not only in the delicious taste but also in the artistry of its preparation, where the skillful rotation and precise cutting by the chef play a crucial role in defining its quality. Döner kebab has transcended its cultural roots to become a symbol of Turkish gastronomy worldwide, embodying the rich culinary traditions and the dynamic street food scene of Turkey. Whether served on a plate with a side of pilaf and grilled vegetables or as a wrap for a meal on the go, Turkish döner kebab continues to enchant food enthusiasts with its delectable layers of flavor and its rich, multicultural history.

Ingredients Needed:

    1 teaspoon all-purpose flour
    1 teaspoon dried oregano
    ½ teaspoon salt
    ½ teaspoon garlic powder
    ½ teaspoon onion powder
    ½ teaspoon dried Italian herb seasoning
    ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
    ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
    1 ¼ pounds ground lamb

List of Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Combine flour, oregano, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Add ground lamb and knead until thoroughly mixed together, about 3 minutes.
  3. Shape seasoned lamb mixture and place into a loaf pan; set on top of a baking sheet.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven, turning halfway to ensure even browning, for about 1 hour and 20 minutes.
  5. Wrap loaf in aluminum foil and let rest for about 10 minutes. Slice as thinly as possible to make doner kebab pieces.
