Turkish Meatballs

Original Source Material

Kofta, a staple in Turkish cuisine, is a delicious and versatile dish made with ground meat, often beef or lamb, mixed with a variety of spices and sometimes onions, garlic, and herbs. This mixture is then shaped into balls or patties and can be cooked in several ways, including grilling, frying, or baking. The unique blend of spices used in kofta, such as cumin, paprika, mint, and parsley, gives it a distinctive flavor that sets it apart from other meatball dishes around the world. It's commonly served with sides of rice or flatbreads, fresh salads, and yogurt or tahini sauce, making it a satisfying and balanced meal.

The origins of kofta are believed to date back to ancient times, with variations of the dish found across Middle Eastern, South Asian, and Balkan cuisines, highlighting the rich culinary exchange in these regions. In Turkey, kofta is more than just a meal; it's a part of the country's culinary heritage and is often enjoyed at gatherings, celebrations, and family dinners. Each region in Turkey might have its own version of kofta, adding local ingredients or altering the preparation method, thereby enriching the diversity of this beloved dish. Whether it's the spicy İzmir köfte, the skewered Adana köfte, or the simple yet flavorful Akçaabat köfte, each variant offers a unique taste experience that reflects the rich tapestry of Turkish culture and cuisine.

Ingredients Needed:

    2 slices slightly stale whole wheat bread
    1 pound lean ground lamb
    1 egg
    2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    2 cloves garlic
    1 teaspoon ground cumin
    1 teaspoon dried mint
    ½ teaspoon ground allspice
    ½ teaspoon salt
    ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
    1 tablespoon olive oil

List of Directions:

  1. Tear up bread slices into the bowl of a food processor; process into fine crumbs. Add ground lamb and egg; process until blended. Add parsley, garlic, cumin, mint, allspice, salt, and pepper; process until evenly combined.

  2. Roll lamb mixture into small meatballs.

  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook meatballs in batches, turning occasionally until browned on all sides, 4 to 5 minutes per batch. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate using a slotted spoon.

Tips:

    Refrigerate meatballs for 1 hour before cooking, if desired.
